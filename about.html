About Me

I am an aspiring computational thermo-fluid researcher with a research focus on using computational methods to understand multiphase and turbulent flow phenomena. More broadly, I am interested in research that sits at the intersection of Computational Fluid Dynamics, High-Performance Computing, and Scientific Machine Learning.

My journey has been shaped by a blend of research, software development, and industry experience. I currently work as a Finite Element Software Developer at Africa Makes, where I contribute to the development of Enitan, Africa's first cloud-based finite element solver. Alongside this, I intern as a Machine Learning Engineer at FlyRank AI, where I contribute to AI-assisted SEO and data analytics solutions using machine learning techniques.

On the research side, I have had the privilege of working in several laboratories, including the Thermal-Fluid Laboratory and the Materials and Ballistic Laboratory at the University of Benin, the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN), and currently the Next Energy Xystems Technologies (NEXT) Research Hub. Across these experiences, I have worked on computational modelling, structural and fluid simulations, numerical analysis, and scientific computing to solve a diverse range of engineering problems.

I am always excited to collaborate on projects involving computational fluid dynamics, scientific machine learning, numerical methods, and high-performance computing. Feel free to explore my portfolio to learn more about my work, and don't hesitate to reach out if you'd like to connect. Outside of engineering, I enjoy playing the bass guitar and continuously improving my German and French speaking abilities.

About Me

I am a budding computational thermo-fluid researcher. My research focuses on using numerical models and simulations to understand multiphase and turbulence flow problems, with a particular interest in cavitation dynamics, an area I have explored extensively through my undergraduate thesis on cavitation in centrifugal pumps. My broader interests sit at the intersection of physics-informed machine learning and data-driven turbulence modelling, where i can use and develop my skills in multiphysics coupling between CFD and FEA, high-performance computing, and the use of machine learning, further pushing these areas of research.

My path here has been shaped by a mix of research and industry experience. I worked as an FEA Analyst and Developer at Africa Makes, a startup building Africa's first cloud-based FEA solver, where I contributed to solver development and structural simulation workflows from the ground up. I currently work as a Systems and Cloud Engineer at FPG Technologies and Solutions, a role that has broadened my technical foundation into high-performance and cloud infrastructure alongside my engineering background. On the research side, I had the opportunity to work in several labs including Thhermal-Fluid Laboratory (University of Benin), European Union for Nuclear Research (CERN), Materials and Ballistic Laboratory (University of Benin), and currently at Next Energy Xystems Technologies (NEXT) Research Hub. My works at these research labs all sits at the intersection of computational modelling, simulation and analysis.

I'm currently open to collaborating on projects, especially those at the intersection of computational mechanics, fluid dynamics, and machine learning. Feel free to explore my profile to get a sense of my work, and don't hesitate to reach out if you'd like to connect!
